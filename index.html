Crystal Defense - A Tower Defense VR Game

Introduction

Our team of five was faced with the challenge of creating a VR game within a three-month timeframe. With the requirement of utilizing cutting-edge VR technology, we set out to deliver an immersive and visually appealing tower defense experience.

Project Scope and Approach

To optimize our limited time while ensuring scalability, we decided to develop a tower defense game. With a focus on the VR medium, we dedicated ourselves to creating a captivating world with custom-made assets, visual effects, and hand-painted textures. For development, we employed Unreal Engine and Visual Studio, alongside Blender and Procreate for visual components. Our implementation involved a combination of C++ programming and Unreal's Blueprint visual scripting language.

My Responsibilities

I took charge of implementing the building system, enabling players to strategically place offensive and defensive towers throughout the virtual world. Additionally, I undertook the creation of enemy models and integrated animations to enhance their behavior and interactions within the game. I was also responsible for painting textures and some of the visual effects.

Enhancing Immersion and Gameplay

Our objective was to create an immersive experience that went beyond the traditional tower defense gameplay of placing towers and passively observing. To achieve this, we implemented shooting mechanics, enabling players to actively engage with the game environment. By incorporating the player as an additional variable within the game world, we aimed to enhance strategic decision-making and foster dynamic gameplay experiences.

Challenges and Solutions

Throughout development, we encountered two key challenges. Firstly, we had to carefully manage performance for the VR platform. Optimizing polygon count, effects, etc. was essential to ensure smooth gameplay and a seamless player experience. Secondly, designing the user interface (UI) for VR proved complex. We needed a UI that moved naturally with the player, allowing intuitive selection without cumbersome menu interactions. While our solutions were sufficient, further improvements would have greatly enhanced gameplay. This underscored the importance of proper UI design and made us rethink our design approach for future projects.

Impact of our Game

We were thrilled to have the opportunity to showcase our game at the GG Bavaria Convention in Munich as well as our university's career day. Our professor entrusted us with representing our university and its game development course. During the events, we also showed the GameLab, a dedicated space equipped with modern hardware for student projects. Game Jam Success: Creating "Another Kind of Date"

Introduction

When my university announced a game jam organized by the Franco-German Youth Office, I applied and secured a spot. I traveled to AngoulÃªme, France, where the game jam commenced with the theme: "climate change." Our challenge was to develop a game concept addressing this issue, catering to students in Germany and France. The game aimed to be a serious educational tool, suitable for classroom use. With limited time, we formed teams comprising German and French university students, ready to bring our vision to fruition.

Game Concept: "Another Kind of Date"

Our project, "Another Kind of Date," materialized as a politically-driven dating simulator. Picture yourself as a mediator, navigating the complexities of international diplomacy by engaging in unconventional "dates" with fictional world leaders. The goal? Persuade them to support climate-friendly legislation. We spent two days refining our concept and seeking feedback through several sales pitches, guided by a jury comprised of professional game designers, climate change experts, education specialists, and competing teams.

Victory and Continued Development

We emerged as the winners of the contest, which provided us with the necessary financial support to work on our project for an additional six months. Our task shifted towards creating a comprehensive Game Design Document, encompassing essential elements such as budget allocation, timeline planning, key features, captivating gameplay mechanics, and strategic partnerships. Within the team, my primary responsibility revolved around overseeing the art direction and working on game design aspects.

Challenges and Lessons Learned

Working with an international team presented its share of challenges. Throughout those six months, we encountered difficulties in coordinating schedules due to academic and work commitments. Text messages became our primary mode of communication, although it wasn't always smooth sailing. However, the mistakes we made taught us invaluable lessons about the significance of effective communication in project management. Now, I am better prepared for future international collaborations.

Portfolio Website

Introduction

Embarking on my very first web development project, I aimed to create a portfolio website that would not only be a learning experience but also make use of newer technologies. Instead of taking the easy route of copying existing portfolio templates, I wanted to make something unique. With my love for both 2D and 3D art, I decided to dive into Three.js to bring 3D models into the mix.

Designing with Stylized 3D

My design process kicked off with me getting my hands dirty in Blender. I created a stylized room as the centerpiece for the website. This room set the tone for the entire design, especially when it came to colors. I wanted it to look good, even as a standalone art piece. So, I had two goals in mind: to make it visually appealing with a pleasing color scheme, and to keep the model's poly count low to ensure smooth performance without sacrificing the artistic flair.

Learning the Basics

Being a total newbie in web development, I had to start from scratch and learn HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. My aim was to grasp the fundamentals and figure out one of the many approaches to web development. But the most important thing was to make it work in the end! Despite the challenges, it was a fun experience, and I'm eager to continue learning and exploring more.

Utilizing Frameworks and Tools

Of course, I couldn't go completely framework-free and tackle everything on my own, since that would have taken far too long. So, I decided to embrace the power of Three.js to handle all the 3D assets, and I enlisted the help of GSAP's scrolltrigger feature to add camera and room animations.

Responsive Design Challenges

After weeks of working on the website on the side, I thought everything was going smoothly. But when I resized the window to a mobile format, I discovered that the website layout faltered when viewed on different devices. It took some tweaking and troubleshooting, especially with the 3D aspects of the website, to make the website look decent on both desktop and mobile devices.
